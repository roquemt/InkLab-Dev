--- [1] SCHEMATIC & COMPONENT TWEAKS ---
[ ] I2C BUS (1.8V): Create a dedicated 1.8V ultra-low-power I2C bus for the MAX30102 (strictly 1.8V logic), LSM6DSO, and SHT4x.
[ ] I2C BUS (3.3V): Route the MLX90632 on a separate 3.3V MCU bus OR use a bidirectional logic level shifter (e.g., PCA9306) to bridge it to the 1.8V bus.
[ ] INTERRUPTS: Connect the hardware FIFO INT (Interrupt) pins from the LSM6DSO and MAX30102 directly to the MCU's EXTI wake-up pins.
[ ] PULL-UPS: Recalculate I2C pull-up resistors for the 1.8V bus (e.g., 2.2kΩ vs 4.7kΩ) to balance rise-time requirements with the new lower voltage.
[ ] VOLTAGE REGULATION: Ensure the PMIC/LDO architecture can supply a clean, dedicated 1.8V rail for the sensors and I2C pull-ups.

--- [2] PCB LAYOUT & ROUTING ---
[ ] TRACE WIDTH: Reduce digital logic traces from 0.20mm down to your manufacturer's reliable minimum (e.g., 0.12mm - 0.15mm) to lower the baseline parallel-plate capacitance to ground.
[ ] EPD ROUTING: Delete all serpentine length-matching traces on the e-ink lines. Route them as straight and directly as possible to reduce total trace length.
[ ] TRACE SPACING: Apply the "3W Rule" (spacing between traces = 3x the trace width) for parallel single-ended buses to minimize mutual capacitance and crosstalk.
[ ] ESD PROTECTION: Maintain the ESD224 chips at the connectors, but ensure there is no through-routing for the data channels  (do not draw traces directly through the component pads; use short stubs to the pads instead).

--- [3] CONNECTOR & PINOUT OPTIMIZATION ---
[ ] EXT CONNECTOR: Reorder pins to isolate high-speed aggressors (SPI SCK, MOSI, MISO) from each other.
[ ] EXT CONNECTOR: Interleave GND pins as electromagnetic shields (e.g., GND - MOSI - GND - SCK - MISO - GND).
[ ] EXT CONNECTOR: Group slow or static control pins (CS, DC, RST) together, as they rarely toggle and will not induce crosstalk.
[ ] EPD HEADER: Apply the exact same GND-interleaving strategy to the fast SPI lines driving the e-ink display.

--- [4] FIRMWARE & LAB TESTING ---

Hardware FIFOs: Rewrite MCU firmware to remain in deep sleep while the MAX30102 and LSM6DSO gather data autonomously, waking only when their buffers are full.

Crosstalk Validation: Re-run the Odd-Mode vs. Even-Mode JS capacitance sweep on the Rev 2 board to quantify the physical improvements of the 3W spacing rule.

I2C Power Profiling: Use the INA monitors to validate the expected ~70% drop in active I2C power consumption on the 1.8V bus.
